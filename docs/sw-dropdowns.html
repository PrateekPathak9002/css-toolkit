To use the sw-dropdown class , first import jquery,swifty_js_toolkit,font awesome , and swifty-css . 
<link rel="stylesheet" href="https://cdnjs.cloudflare.com/ajax/libs/font-awesome/5.15.4/css/all.min.css">
<script src="https://code.jquery.com/jquery-2.1.0.js"</script>
<script src="https://unpkg.com/swifty_js_toolkit@1.1.6/swifty.js"</script>
<link rel="stylesheet" href="https://unpkg.com/swifty-css@1.1.6/swifty.css">
Now to use the dropdowns , we make a div that contains all the dropdowns of one class. For example , if you have a FAQ section that has multiple sections in it , and those sections have multiple questions it them , then this is extremely useful.So let's do just that . Create a div with class sw-dropdowns .Make sure it is plural . 
<div class="sw-dropdowns"></div>
Now inside this , we need to create multiple questions or dropdowns . So use the class sw-dropdown . Make sure it's singular 
<div class="sw-dropdowns">
	<div class="sw-dropdown"></div>
</div>
Next add a question or dropdown title , depending on your use . 
<div class="sw-dropdowns">
	<div class="sw-dropdown">
		<div class="head">Wait ! Don't run it yet . We still need to add 1 more class to this div. </div>
	</div>
</div>
Now , depending on your use , you may want the dropdown arrow on a specific side . So simply give the head div another class of left or right. NOTE: This class is required. 
<div class="sw-dropdowns">
	<div class="sw-dropdown">
		<div class="head right"> Hello ,world !</div>
	</div>
</div>
Next we can prepare to add the options to the dropdown or the answer to a FAQ .Add the sw-dropdown-body class to a new div. 
<div class="sw-dropdowns">
	<div class="sw-dropdown">
		<div class="head right">Hello ,world ! </div>
		<div class="sw-dropdown-body"></div>
	</div>
</div>
Then if you want options , then add multiple divs with class sw-dropdown-option inside the sw-dropdown-body. 
<div class="sw-dropdowns">
	<div class="sw-dropdown">
		<div class="head right">Hello ,world ! </div>
		<div class="sw-dropdown-body"> 
			<div class="sw-dropdown-option">Item1 </div>
			<div class="sw-dropdown-option">Item2 </div>
			<div class="sw-dropdown-option">Item3 </div>
			<div class="sw-dropdown-option">Item4 </div>
		</div>

	</div>
</div>
That's for the dropdown . Now the FAQ . Adding a paragraph to the dropdown/accordian is fairly simple . Simply replace the sw-dropdown-option with a p element. 
<div class="sw-dropdowns">
	<div class="sw-dropdown">
		<div class="head right">Hello ,world ! </div>
		<div class="sw-dropdown-body"> 
			<p>Hi , I'm the answer to a FAQ question . </p>
		</div>

	</div>
</div>
